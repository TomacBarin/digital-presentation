Why Fullstack?

Why Chas?

[ Two threads pull me here. ]

Professional.

Despite the fact that there's never been a better time to become a developer — with the job market literally exploding with opportunities at prestigious tech firms — I'm drawn to the intimate world of small, passionate companies selling niche treasures, like obscure films, indie games, arcade pinball parts - or even unique clothing. These outfits often run lean, with everyone wearing multiple hats, and I thrive in that collaborative chaos. Take HepCat, a store specializing in distinctive retro fashion and rare vinyl finds — an inspiring example of the quirky businesses I'd love to support.
The Chas Academy Fullstack Developer Program equips me to become their one-person web powerhouse, crafting responsive sites while managing backend systems. Inspired by solo developers who've built vibrant e-commerce platforms for niche markets, I'm eager to code solutions that keep these hidden gems thriving online.

Personal.

I am one skill away from creating my first shmup. With a background in 3D art and sound design, programming is the final piece I need to bring my vision to life. The Chas Academy Fullstack Developer Program allows me to master JavaScript, which I'll combine with Phaser for my initial prototypes, before advancing to C# and Unity to craft full-blown bullet hell shmups. Drawing inspiration from the dynamic intensity of Crimzon Clover and the creative flair of Angel at Dusk, both created by solo developers, I'm excited to code my own games.


So there.
